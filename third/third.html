Insomnolence








I navigate the dark house by moving from the green star of the smoke detector to the blue star of the electric toothbrush.
I am no different than Magellan or Marco Polo, I am guided by what burns. Some nights I step onto the back porch.
The prow of it charges the blackness, while the stars above me sharpen and blur.
Inside, I harbor the ache of what is no longer possible.
